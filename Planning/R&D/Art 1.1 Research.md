> [!HELP]- Purpose and Rational of Method
> Each disputation ends with a judgement and aims at understanding. Therefore, using the three stages of understanding make for a good method.
> 
> Consider the following argument:
> > If A then B;
> > But B;
> > Therefore A.
> 
> A is the statement we want to make (Socrates is mortal). B is condition for A (Socrates can and has died). The major premise is the real insight, the connection between the terms. The minor premise is a matter of fact. The conclusion is the final judgement of the statement we want to make.
> 
> This information gathering format (heuristic even) is based around finding support for each premise
> 
> Note that this is written for a judgement of fact which includes experience of reality. However, it can be adapted for analytic statements by replacing data with a definition.
